One Mil-Lion - A Song of Pride and Fire

One Mil-Lion is a Top-down Shooter game where you play as a Fire-breathing Dragon in a colosseum. In this colosseum, however, you must take on a growing horde of lions, perhaps one that grows up to a mil-lion, and survive as long as you can.

This was the first Game Jam that I joined and was hosted by Georgia Tech's Video Game Development Club. As this Jam lasted 48-hours and there were five of us on the team, we spent about an hour coming up with ideas once the theme of the Jam, "All Around You", came out that were doable in that short of a time frame. We wanted a funny, lighthearted idea and settled on this idea after having a debate on whether a dragon could beat a million lions.

We then listed out tasks needed to be done and took on what we each believed could do best. I initially took writing an enemy spawner script. Once I finished that, I took on UI programming and created the Game Over Screen. After finishing this, we needed audio so I found and created sound effects, which I then implemented in game. After accomplishing this, the Jam concluded and we finished everything we wanted for the Minimum Viable Product.

Overall, this was a really good experience as I had the opportunity to work in a great team, all of whom are still good friends of mine. It also gave me an idea of how to best scope out projects based on deadlines. I also learned more about the different features of Unity such as creating UI and the Engine's Audio Manager. The game won the Judges' Choice Award and received positive feedback overall from people who had the chance to play it.

Project info

  • Engine: Unity, C#
  • Positions: Programmer, Audio/SFX
