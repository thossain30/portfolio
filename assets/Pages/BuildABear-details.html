Build-A-Bear - A logo Remake

For this project, we had to take a logo from a company and make a new one to give the company a more modern look. I decided to go with Build-a-Bear since their logo seemed a bit outdated to me.

At first, I went for an oversimplification of the bear as shown in the top left variation as my initial thought was that the bear in the current logo looked too complex and added the phrases that I thought were most important to the company. I also decided to change the font and replaced with a more fun, friendly and easier to read font.

After some variations where I experimented with other features including even changing it from a Grizzly Bear to a Panda Bear, I ended up with the Logo on the second slide after combining the parts of the variations that I liked the most and adding slightly more details.

During the feedback session, I received valuable and constructive advice from my peers that really helped form the final logo. First piece of advice I got was that the bottom text was taking up too much space on the logo considering it isn't as significant as the other elements of the logo so I decreased the size of it. Another piece of advice was to make the bear more "fuzzy" to really sell the fun and friendly environment of the company. I did this by adding more fuzzy lines while trying not to overdo this.

The last piece of advice was to add something that would showcase the customizability of their bears i.e. some piece of clothing. This is a thought that never would have came across my mind which showed me how important having an outside pair of eyes to look at your work is, regardless of what field it is. I opted to add a cute fedora on the bear to do this and it made the logo look a lot nicer to me.

Overall, I am really happy with the end result and it was a good way to learn Adobe Illustrator and the Adobe Suite overall.

Project info
