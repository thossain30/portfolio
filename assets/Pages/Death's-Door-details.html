Death's Door - Accepting Your Own Death

Deathâ€™s Door is a narrative-driven game where you, the player, are about to pass on to the next world. However, before doing so, you must complete a series of trials, games, shaped around memories of the life that you have lived. In doing so, discover the life that you lived as you learn to accept your upcoming death.

This was the first project that I myself led. In doing so, I faced new challenges I did not expect. The first challenge was being able to clearly explain my vision for the project, not just to programmers, but also to artists, writers, and musicians, which led me to having to find different ways of explaining my vision to better help everyone understand. The second challenge was managing tasks needed to be done at the end of a sprint. We were all students and, therefore, were busy with other obligations so I had to take that into account when making sure that the project was going to be in a playable state by the time the demo deadline arrived. The third challenge was facing bugs that came up when adding in a new feature. I spent a long time debugging code from both me and my team to using Visual Studio's debugging tools.

Overall, this is a really fun project as I had the opportunity to turn an idea that I brewed in my head into a playable game. It also gave me a glimpse of Project Management and why Agile Methodology is so valuable when it comes to developing software as it helped me have a successful demo showcase. I also learned valuable skills like debugging and honed other skills such as collaborative programming and version control.
